PAV BHAJI

Pav Bhaji is popular Indian street food where dinner rolls/buns are served with spicy mashed veggies topped with dollop of butter. Street food doesn’t get better than this! This Pav Bhaji Recipe is spicy, so flavorful and can be easily made vegan by replacing butter with vegan butter. If you have never had this in your life, you are seriously missing out!

Ingredients

  1. 2 tablespoons butter (or oil)
  2. 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  3. 1 medium onion (finely chopped)
  4. 1 medium capsicum (bell pepper) (finely chopped)
  5. 2 medium tomatoes (finely chopped or pureed)
  6. 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  7. 1/2 cup green peas (boiled or frozen)
  8. 2 medium potatoes (boiled and mashed)
  9. 1/2 cup cauliflower florets (boiled and mashed)
  10. 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  11. 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  12. 1 1/2 tablespoons pav bhaji masala
  13. 1/2 to 1 cup water (to adjust the consistency)
  14. 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  15. 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves (for garnish)
  16. Pav

Steps

  1. Prepare the Base: Heat 2 tablespoons butter in a large pan. Add 1 teaspoon cumin seeds and let them splutter. Stir in 1 finely chopped onion and sauté until golden. Add 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste and sauté until the raw aroma fades. Toss in 2 finely chopped tomatoes and cook until soft and mushy.
  2. Cook the Vegetables: Add 1 medium chopped capsicum, 1/2 cup boiled green peas, 2 boiled and mashed potatoes, and 1/2 cup boiled and mashed cauliflower florets. Mix well and mash the vegetables using a potato masher. Sprinkle 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder, 1 teaspoon red chili powder, and 1 1/2 tablespoons pav bhaji masala, then cook for 2–3 minutes, letting the spices blend in.
  3. Simmer and Adjust Consistency: Pour 1/2 to 1 cup water to achieve your desired consistency. Mix thoroughly and let the bhaji simmer for 8–10 minutes, stirring occasionally. Add salt to taste and a splash of lemon juice.
  4. Toast the Pav: Heat a tawa or pan and melt 2 tablespoons butter. Sprinkle 1/4 teaspoon pav bhaji masala (optional) and toast the pav buns on both sides until golden and slightly crispy.
  5. Serve and Garnish: Transfer the hot bhaji to a serving bowl and garnish with chopped coriander leaves, a dollop of butter, and chopped onions. Serve with the toasted pav, lemon wedges, and extra onions on the side.
