VEG BALL MANCHURIAN

A Manchurian is one of the most popular Indo-Chinese dishes in India. Manchurian Gravy pairs equally good with a simple noodle dish or even a flavorful fried rice. This post has the Veg Manchurian recipe, which is simply crispy fried mixed vegetable balls in a sweet, spicy, tangy, umami manchurian gravy or sauce. Try it, as I’m sure once you make it at home, you’ll forget the ones served at restaurants.

Ingredients

  1. 1 cup grated cabbage
  2. 1/2 cup grated carrots
  3. 1/4 cup chopped capsicum
  4. 1/4 cup chopped spring onions (white part)
  5. 2 tbsp cornflour, 3 tbsp all-purpose flour
  6. 1/2 tsp ginger-garlic paste, salt, pepper
  7. Oil (for frying)
  8. 1 tbsp oil, 1 tsp chopped garlic & ginger
  9. 2 green chilies, 1/4 cup spring onions
  10. 2 tbsp soy sauce, 1 tbsp ketchup, 1 tsp chili sauce
  11. 1 tsp vinegar, 1 tsp cornflour (dissolved)
  12. Salt, pepper, 1/2 cup water
  13. Spring onion greens

Steps

  1. Prepare the Veg Balls: In a large mixing bowl, combine grated vegetables (like cabbage, carrot, and beans), ginger-garlic paste, chopped green chilies, and cornflour. Add salt, pepper, and a pinch of garam masala. Mix everything well, shape the mixture into small balls, and deep fry them in hot oil until golden brown. Drain on paper towels.
  2. Prepare the Manchurian Sauce: In a pan, heat some oil, then sauté chopped onions, green chilies, and ginger-garlic paste. Add soy sauce, tomato ketchup, vinegar, and a bit of water. Mix well and bring the sauce to a boil.
  3. Combine the Veg Balls and Sauce: Add the fried veg balls to the sauce and toss gently to coat them. Simmer for a few minutes until the sauce thickens and the balls absorb the flavors.
  4. Garnish and Serve: Garnish the Veg Ball Manchurian with chopped spring onions and serve hot with rice or noodles.
